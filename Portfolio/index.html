"The three great essentials to achieving anything worthwhile are: hard work, stick-to-itiveness, and common sense." -Thomas Edison

"Success is no accident. It is hard work, perseverance, learning, studying, sacrifice, and most of all, love of what you are doing or learning to do." -Pele

I hold these truths to be self-evident: That the greatest thing in life is to be doing, to be creating, and to be learning. One must never stagnate, but look to the future, to what could be, what should be, and achieve that. There is no greater gift in this life than to know you are building success for yourself and those you support, that your work is meaningful and impactful, and that you are a valued part of a great team.

I am new to development, but not new to the challenges that arise, nor to the skills needed to overcome them. I have worn many hats over the years, from electronic technician, automotive mechanic, marine mechanic, sales manager, hiring manager, and others. In all things I have strived to be the best, for how could you do less than your best and consider yourself an asset? I have years of knowledge and experience in many walks of life, many industries and many disciplines. I have always been the hardest worker in the room, and that has been the secret to my success. You can give too much effort, and you can never stop learning. In all things, be learning.




“All growth depends upon activity. There is no development physically or intellectually without effort, and effort means work.” – Calvin Coolidge

We come into this world with a small amount of raw material. What we make of that opportunity is entirely of our own effort and ingenuity. Intellect is not the cornerstone of success, effort is; however it takes a certain amount of awareness to channel that effort. You can only win by taking a lesson from the Joshua Tree- when all else fails, hang on and tough it out.
